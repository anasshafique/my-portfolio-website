WHO I AM

About Me

My journey into Computer Science started with a strong curiosity about how technology actually works behind the scenes. During my engineering foundation year, I developed a solid analytical mindset through mathematics and physics, which naturally led me toward software development. Since then, I've enjoyed challenging myself with new concepts, solving problems step by step, and continuously pushing my skills further. I've become comfortable working with HTML, CSS, and C#, and I'm currently expanding my knowledge by learning Python and JavaScript. I see learning as a long-term journey, and I genuinely enjoy the process of improving every day.

Being involved in the tech community has become an important part of my growth. I currently serve as an Events Manager for both the Developers Society on campus and GDG Sunderland, where I help organise workshops, coding sessions, and technical events that bring people together to learn and share ideas. I'm also a PASS Leader, supporting first-year Computer Science students through mentoring sessions, study guidance, and interactive learning activities. These experiences have helped me grow as a communicator and leader, and they've shown me how powerful collaboration and support can be in helping others build confidence and succeed.

Looking ahead, I want to develop into a software engineer who builds meaningful, user-focused technology that solves real problems. I'm especially motivated by opportunities that challenge me to think creatively, work with talented people, and apply my skills in practical environments. My long-term goal is to grow into a versatile developer who can adapt, innovate, and contribute to projects that make a genuine difference in people's lives.
